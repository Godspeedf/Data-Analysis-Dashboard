## ☕ Starbucks Sales Analytics Dashboard (Power BI)
# Project Objective
This repository contains a complete business intelligence project built in Power BI, using a sample Starbucks dataset. The dashboard provides insights into sales performance, customer buying patterns, product trends, and city-level performance across 2015.

## Dataset used
- <a href="https://github.com/Godspeedf/Data-Analysis-Dashboard/blob/main/starbucks.csv"> Starbucks Dataset</a>

## Questions (KPIs)
- Total Revenue
- Average Order Value
- Total Items Sold
- Total Orders
- Average Items Per Order

## ⭐ Project Insights 
- Total Revenue reaches 25.77M, showing strong overall sales performance across the year.
- Average Order Value (AOV) is 1.21K, indicating customers generally purchase higher-value combinations.
- Total Orders recorded are 21,350, with 49,574 items sold, reflecting high product turnover.
- Average Items Per Order is 2.32, suggesting customers often buy more than one item per visit.
- Highest order activity occurs from Thursday to Saturday, making these the peak business days.
- January, March, and December are the busiest months, likely due to seasonal and festive demand.
- Coffee and Bakery categories generate the maximum revenue and order volume, highlighting them as Starbucks strongest product lines.
- Bengaluru contributes the highest share of total sales, making it the most profitable city.
- Cappuccino is the top revenue-generating item, while Blueberry Muffin leads in total quantity and total orders.
- Sandwich emerges as the weakest item, contributing the least in revenue, quantity, and orders.
- Category distribution shows Coffee contributes ~47% of all sales, followed by Bakery.
- City-wise analysis reveals Bengaluru with ~39% sales contribution, followed by Mumbai, New Delhi, and Kolkata.
- Daily and monthly trends show fluctuating order patterns, suggesting predictable cycles that can support staffing and inventory planning.
- Top 5 and Bottom 5 item visuals enable quick identification of best and worst performers, helping in decision-making for promotions or product discontinuation.
